Before getting into what's in the bottle, it helps to understand where Dr. Sturm comes from, because it matters for context. What's Actually in the Formula

This serum is built around five peptides and a supporting amino acid complex. Each one has a specific job, and together they're meant to work as a coordinated system rather than a handful of ingredients thrown together for label appeal.

🧬

Octapeptide-3

The serum's standout ingredient. It relaxes surface-level muscle micro-tensions, producing a mild tightening effect sometimes described as Botox-adjacent. Not quite the same thing, but the mechanism is real.

🌿

Hexapeptide-3

Targets skin density, which is one of the first things to decline as skin ages and becomes thinner and more crepey. Supports visible restoration of that density over time.

✨

Decapeptide-4

A multi-tasker that supports the formation of collagen, elastin, and fibronectin simultaneously, strengthening the overall structure of the skin and reducing the appearance of wrinkles.

💜

Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4

Reduces inflammation, refines texture, and helps strengthen the skin barrier. One of the better-studied peptides in anti-aging formulations, with solid evidence behind it.

💧

Amino Acid Complex + Hydrators

Glycine, proline, and arginine provide the raw building blocks collagen needs to form. Backed by hyaluronic acid, glycerin, shea butter, sunflower oil, and vitamin E to keep the skin barrier intact throughout.

Dr. Sturm's Signature: Purslane Extract

No Sturm formula is complete without purslane extract, an anti-oxidant-rich plant ingredient she calls a cornerstone of her anti-inflammatory approach. It protects against oxidative damage, which is one of the underlying drivers of visible skin aging. It's a small addition but a consistent thread across the entire Molecular Cosmetics range.

A Note on Brand-Conducted Studies

These are the brand's own clinical trials, not independent third-party peer-reviewed research. That's true of almost every skincare brand's clinical claims. The numbers are meaningful — especially the hydration and elasticity improvements, which align with what independent dermatologists say about peptides broadly — but they should be read with that context in mind. What they tell you is that this formulation was tested rigorously, not just released on marketing claims alone.

The Collagen Banking Angle

Dr. Sturm frames the serum around a concept she calls "collagen banking" — the idea that preserving your collagen and elastin reserves in your 30s and 40s pays dividends in how your skin looks and behaves in your 50s and beyond. This is a real and increasingly discussed principle in dermatology. Peptides are one of the better tools for it, and this serum is one of the more thoroughly formulated peptide products on the market. If you're thinking long-term rather than chasing an immediate overnight result, the logic holds.

How to Use It

Simple application, and it stacks well into almost any existing routine. No special timing or complicated layering rules required.

  1. 1
    Cleanse first. Start with a clean face. Peptides work at the surface level as signaling molecules, so removing the day's buildup gives them the best access to the skin.
  2. 2
    Dispense a full dropper into your palm. The milky texture spreads easily. Press it gently into the face, neck, and décolleté rather than rubbing.
  3. 3
    Give it 30 seconds. It absorbs quickly. The skin should feel slightly plumped and smooth — not greasy or heavy — before moving to the next step.
  4. 4
    Layer your moisturizer on top. Dr. Sturm naturally recommends pairing it with one of her own face creams, but it works well under any moisturizer. Follow with SPF in the morning.
  5. 5
    Be patient for 2 to 4 weeks. Most reviewers noticed changes between 10 days and 3 weeks in. Peptides support a biological process — they're not like a hyaluronic acid filler that plumps immediately on contact. The results are real, just not instant.
